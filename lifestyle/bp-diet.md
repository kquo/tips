## Blood Pressure Diet
Blood pressure diet cheat-sheet.

### Quick Tips
- Remember that the [kidneys are the body’s primary long-term regulators of blood pressure](kidneys-bp.md).
- Choose fresh or minimally processed foods.
- Rinse canned beans and vegetables to reduce sodium.
- Use herbs, spices, lemon, and vinegar instead of salt.
- Aim for mostly home-cooked meals.

### ✔ Foods That Help Lower or Prevent High Blood Pressure

| Category | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| **Potassium-Rich Foods** | Bananas, oranges & citrus, avocados, tomatoes, beans & lentils, sweet potatoes, spinach, kale, Swiss chard |
| **Vegetables & Greens** | Beets / beet juice, arugula, broccoli, mixed vegetables |
| **Whole Grains** | Oats, quinoa, whole-grain breads & cereals |
| **Healthy Fats** | Olive oil, nuts & seeds, salmon, sardines, mackerel |
| **Low-Fat Dairy** | Yogurt, kefir, low-fat milk |

### ✖ Foods to Limit or Avoid

| Category | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| **High-Sodium Foods** | Canned soups, frozen meals, fast food, deli meats, bacon, sausage, soy sauce, teriyaki sauce |
| **Processed Snacks** | Chips, instant noodles, packaged baked goods |
| **Sugary Foods & Drinks** | Soda, desserts, sweetened coffee drinks |
| **Excess Alcohol & Caffeine** | More than 1–2 drinks/day, excess energy drinks or coffee (if BP-sensitive) |
| **Unhealthy Fats** | Fried foods, processed meats, foods containing trans fats |
